Smoky Three-Bean Chipotle Chili

Thick, smoky, high-protein chili built for big appetite, toppings, and freezing in bricks.

Serves 6–8 · Prep: 15 min · Cook: 30–40 min

GF Vegetarian Easily Vegan High-Protein Freezer-Friendly Meal prep OK

Ingredients

Base
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 1 large carrot, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
Spices & flavour
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½–1 tsp ground coriander (optional)
  • 1–2 chipotle peppers in adobo, minced
  • 1 tsp adobo sauce from the can
  • 1 tsp sugar or maple syrup
  • Salt & black pepper, to taste
Liquids & beans
  • 2 cans (14 oz / 398 ml) crushed or diced tomatoes
  • 1 can black beans, drained & rinsed
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained & rinsed
  • 1 can pinto or navy beans, drained & rinsed
  • 2–3 cups vegetable broth
  • Juice of 1 lime
To serve (optional)
  • Shredded cheese, sour cream or yogurt
  • Sliced green onions, chopped cilantro
  • Avocado, crushed GF tortilla chips

Method

  1. Heat oil in a large heavy pot over medium. Add onion, carrot, celery, and bell pepper. Cook 8–10 minutes until softened and lightly golden.
  2. Stir in garlic and cook 1 minute. Add tomato paste and all spices. Cook 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add chipotle, adobo, and tomatoes, scraping up any browned bits.
  4. Add beans, 2 cups broth, sugar or maple, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, then simmer on low 30–40 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add more broth if too thick.
  6. Adjust seasoning; stir in lime juice at the end.
  7. Serve with toppings. Even better the next day; freezes well.

Vegan: Use plant-based toppings or skip dairy.

Storage: 4–5 days in the fridge, 3 months in the freezer.

Nutrition & usage ▼

How to use it:

  • Storage: Fridge 3–4 days; freezer up to 2–3 months in sealed containers.
  • Reheat: Stovetop over low heat with a splash of water/stock; or microwave in short bursts, stirring.
  • Serve with: GF bread / flatbread, simple salad, roasted veg, or over rice/quinoa for extra bulk. Try Quick GF Yogurt Skillet Flatbreads or GF Skillet Cornbread from this book.
  • Variations: Adjust spice and thickness with extra stock or coconut milk; easy to add beans or fake meat at the end.
  • Meal prep: Keeps well for 3–4 days in the fridge in sealed containers. Let it cool fully before lid goes on, and reheat gently with a splash of water or broth if it looks dry.
  • Regular gluten / non-vegetarian option (for meal prep): For mixed eaters, keep the base as written and cook meat once in a separate pan. Plan about 350–400 g (12–14 oz) cooked chicken, sausage, or lean ground meat per full recipe batch. Use regular wheat pasta / bread / tortillas where the recipe calls for GF versions, and spoon the meat over individual portions so you don’t need a second pot of sauce.

Creamy Roasted Cauliflower & White Bean Soup

Roasted cauliflower blended with white beans and broth into a silky, satisfying soup that actually fills you up.

Serves 4–6 · Prep: 10–15 min · Cook: 25–30 min

GF Vegetarian Easily Vegan Creamy Meal prep OK

Ingredients

Soup base
  • 1 large head cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 3 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • Salt & pepper
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika (optional)
  • 1 can (14 oz) white beans, drained & rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • ½–1 cup milk or cream (or unsweetened plant milk)
  • 1–2 tbsp nutritional yeast or grated Parmesan (optional)
  • Juice of ½ lemon
To finish
  • Extra olive oil, for drizzling
  • Chopped parsley or chives
  • Toasted pumpkin seeds (optional)

Method

  1. Heat oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss cauliflower with 2 tbsp oil, salt, and pepper. Roast 25–30 minutes, turning once.
  2. Sauté onion in remaining 1 tbsp oil 7–8 minutes. Add garlic, thyme, smoked paprika; cook 1 minute.
  3. Add roasted cauliflower, beans, and broth. Simmer 10–15 minutes.
  4. Blend until smooth (stick blender or regular blender in batches).
  5. Stir in milk/cream and nutritional yeast/Parmesan. Warm gently 5 minutes.
  6. Add lemon juice; adjust salt and pepper.
  7. Serve with olive oil, herbs, and seeds.

Vegan: Use plant milk and nutritional yeast.

Storage: 3–4 days in the fridge; loosen with water when reheating.

Hearty Red Lentil & Vegetable Soup

Thick, tomatoey red lentil soup packed with vegetables. High-protein, cosy, and built for meal prep.

Serves 6 · Prep: 15 min · Cook: 30–35 min

GF Vegetarian Easily Vegan High-Protein Meal-Prep Friendly Meal prep OK

Ingredients

Base
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 red or yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 3–4 cloves garlic, minced
Spices & flavour
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp ground coriander (optional)
  • ¼–½ tsp chili flakes (optional)
  • Salt & black pepper, to taste
Liquids & lentils
  • 1½ cups dried red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz / 398 ml) diced tomatoes
  • 6 cups vegetable broth (up to 7 cups for thinner soup)
  • 1–2 tsp lemon juice or red wine vinegar
Finish
  • 2–3 cups chopped greens (spinach, kale, or Swiss chard)
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (optional)
  • Olive oil or plain yogurt to drizzle (optional)

Method

  1. Cook onion, carrots, celery, and pepper in oil 8–10 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and spices; cook 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add lentils, tomatoes, 6 cups broth, salt, and pepper.
  4. Simmer 20–25 minutes, stirring, until lentils are soft and soup is thick. Add broth if needed.
  5. Stir in greens; cook 3–5 minutes.
  6. Add lemon or vinegar; adjust seasoning.
  7. Serve with olive oil/yogurt and herbs.

Texture: Lightly mash or pulse-blend if you want it creamier.

Storage: 4–5 days in the fridge; 3 months in the freezer.

Creamy Potato, Leek & Kale Soup

Classic potato–leek soup with extra greens and protein from white beans. Comfort in a bowl.

Serves 4–6 · Prep: 15 min · Cook: 25–30 min

GF Vegetarian Easily Vegan Creamy Meal prep OK

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp butter or olive oil
  • 2 large leeks, white and light green parts sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 can (14 oz) white beans, drained & rinsed
  • 5 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups chopped kale (stems removed)
  • ½–1 cup milk, cream, or plant milk
  • Salt & black pepper
  • Juice of ½ lemon (optional)

Method

  1. Sauté leeks in butter/oil over medium 8–10 minutes until soft but not browned.
  2. Add garlic; cook 1 minute.
  3. Add potatoes, beans, and broth. Bring to a simmer; cook 15–20 minutes until potatoes are tender.
  4. Blend about half the soup and return for a chunky-creamy texture.
  5. Stir in kale and milk/cream; simmer 5 minutes until kale is tender.
  6. Season with salt, pepper, and lemon juice if desired.

Vegan: Use olive oil and plant milk.

Serve with: GF toast, grated cheese on top, or a drizzle of olive oil.

Spiced Butternut Squash & Apple Soup

Roasted squash and apple blended with warm spices into a smooth, cosy soup.

Serves 4 · Prep: 15 min · Cook: 35–40 min

GF Vegetarian Vegan Silky Meal prep OK

Ingredients

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled, seeded, cubed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • Salt & pepper
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 apples (any tart-sweet), peeled & chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • ½–1 cup coconut milk or cream (optional)

Method

  1. Heat oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss squash with 1 tbsp oil, salt, and pepper; roast 25–30 minutes until soft and caramelised.

  3. Add garlic, cumin, cinnamon, and nutmeg; cook 1 minute.
  4. Add roasted squash and broth; simmer 10 minutes.
  5. Blend until smooth; stir in coconut milk or cream if using. Adjust seasoning.

One-Pot Creamy Tomato & Lentil Pasta

Everything cooks in one pot: GF pasta, red lentils, tomatoes, and broth finished with a splash of cream or cashew cream.

Serves 4 · Prep: 10 min · Cook: 20–25 min

GF Vegetarian One pot High Protein Meal prep OK

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp chili flakes (optional)
  • 1 cup dried red lentils, rinsed
  • 300–350 g GF short pasta (fusilli, penne, etc.)
  • 1 can (398 ml) crushed tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth (plus extra splash if needed)
  • 1/3–1/2 cup cream, milk, or cashew cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan or nutritional yeast
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh basil or parsley to finish

Method

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium. Sauté onion 5–7 minutes until soft.
  2. Add garlic, oregano, basil, and chili flakes; cook 1 minute.
  3. Stir in lentils, dry pasta, crushed tomatoes, and broth. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  4. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce to a lively simmer. Stir often so pasta doesn’t stick. Cook 12–15 minutes until pasta and lentils are tender; add a splash of broth if it gets too thick.
  5. Stir in cream/cashew cream and Parmesan/nutritional yeast. Adjust salt, pepper, and chili; rest off heat 5 minutes before serving with herbs.

How to use it:

  • Meal prep: Holds 3 days; loosen with a splash of water/broth when reheating.
  • Regular gluten / non-veg option: Non-veg person can have regular pasta cooked separately, or add 80–100 g cooked sausage/chicken to their portion only.

Mushroom & Lentil Shepherd’s Pie

Deeply savoury lentil and mushroom filling under a thick layer of golden mashed potatoes.

Serves 6–8 · Prep: 25–30 min · Bake: 25–30 min

GF Vegetarian Easily Vegan Freezer-Friendly Meal prep OK

Ingredients

Potato topping
  • 1.5 kg (about 3 lb) potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 3–4 tbsp butter or olive oil
  • ½–¾ cup milk or plant milk
  • Salt & pepper
Filling
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 400 g (about 14 oz) mushrooms, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1½ cups dried green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tbsp GF tamari or soy sauce
  • 4 cups vegetable broth (plus a bit more if needed)
  • 1–2 tsp Worcestershire-style sauce (GF/veg or skip)
  • Salt & black pepper

Method

  1. Boil potatoes in salted water 15–20 minutes until tender. Drain; mash with butter/oil and milk. Season.
  2. Sauté onion, carrots, celery in oil 8–10 minutes.
  3. Add mushrooms and salt; cook 8–10 minutes until most liquid evaporates.
  4. Add garlic, lentils, tomato paste, herbs, smoked paprika, and tamari; cook 1–2 minutes.
  5. Add broth and Worcestershire; simmer covered 25–30 minutes, adding more liquid if needed, until lentils are tender and thick.
  6. Season filling; transfer to baking dish.
  7. Top with potatoes; rough up surface with a fork.
  8. Bake at 400°F (200°C) 25–30 minutes until golden and bubbling. Rest 10 minutes.

Vegan: Use olive oil and plant milk in mash; vegan Worcestershire or omit.

Nutrition & usage ▼

How to use it:

  • Storage: Fridge 3–4 days; many mains freeze well in meal-sized containers.
  • Reheat: Oven or stovetop over medium-low heat; add a splash of water or sauce if it looks dry.
  • Serve with: Side salad, steamed greens, or roasted veg; add bread/flatbread or rice for heavier appetites.
  • Variations: Swap beans/lentils ↔ fake meat; for meat-eaters, cook meat separately and fold into their portions at the end.
  • Meal prep: Keeps well for 3–4 days in the fridge in sealed containers. Let it cool fully before lid goes on, and reheat gently with a splash of water or broth if it looks dry.
  • Regular gluten / non-vegetarian option (for meal prep): For mixed eaters, keep the base as written and cook meat once in a separate pan. Plan about 350–400 g (12–14 oz) cooked chicken, sausage, or lean ground meat per full recipe batch. Serves 4–5 · Prep: 20 min · Cook: 30–35 min

GF Vegetarian Sheet Pan Meal prep OK

Ingredients

  • 1 medium cauliflower, florets
  • 3 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained & rinsed
  • 1 red onion, wedges
  • 1 cup plain yogurt or thick plant yogurt
  • 2 tbsp tandoori spice blend (ensure GF)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • Salt & pepper
  • Fresh cilantro for topping

Method

  1. Heat oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a large tray.
  2. Whisk yogurt, spices, oil, lemon, salt, and pepper.
  3. Toss cauliflower, potatoes, chickpeas, and onion in marinade; spread on tray.
  4. Roast 30–35 minutes, stirring once, until browned and tender.
  5. Top with cilantro and serve with rice or GF naan-style bread.

  • Variations: Swap beans/lentils ↔ fake meat; for meat-eaters, cook meat separately and fold into their portions at the end.

GF Vegetarian Casserole Meal prep OK

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained & rinsed
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp GF flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2½ cups shredded cheddar
  • ½ tsp mustard powder or 1 tsp Dijon
  • Salt & pepper

Method

  4. Mix rice, broccoli, chickpeas, and cheese sauce; transfer to dish.
  5. Top with remaining cheese. Mix breadcrumbs with melted butter and sprinkle on top if using.
  6. Bake 25–30 minutes until bubbling and golden.

Blueberry Lemon Baked Oat Squares (GF)

Soft, sliceable baked oats loaded with blueberries and lemon, built for grab-and-go breakfasts.

Makes 9–12 squares · Prep: 15 min · Bake: 30–35 min

GF Vegetarian Oven Meal prep OK

Ingredients

  • 2 cups gluten-free rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup ground flaxseed or chia seed
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder (GF)
  • 1/4 tsp fine salt
  • 2 cups milk or unsweetened plant milk
  • 2 large eggs, or 2 flax eggs for vegan
  • 1/3–1/2 cup maple syrup or brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp melted butter or neutral oil
  • Zest of 1 large lemon
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 1/2 cups blueberries (fresh or frozen, no need to thaw)
  • Optional: 1 tsp vanilla extract

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease or line an 8×8 inch (or similar) baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, combine oats, ground flax, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together milk, eggs (or flax eggs), maple syrup, melted butter, lemon zest, lemon juice, and vanilla if using.
  4. Pour wet mixture into the dry ingredients and stir until combined. Fold in the blueberries.
  5. Pour into the baking dish, spread evenly, and bake 30–35 minutes until the center is set and the top is lightly golden.
  6. Cool completely before slicing for meal prep; store covered in the fridge.
Nutrition & usage ▼

How to use it:

  • Meal prep: Cool completely, cut into squares, and store in the fridge up to 4 days. Reheat briefly or eat cold.
  • Serve with: Yogurt, nut butter, extra berries, or a boiled egg on the side.
  • Regular gluten / non-vegetarian option (for meal prep): For a non-GF person, you can make a second small pan with regular oats if tolerated, and serve theirs with regular toast or a breakfast sausage on the side (about 1–2 sausages, 60–80 g cooked).

Warm Lentil & Roasted Carrot Bowl

Roasted carrots and onions over garlicky lentils with a bright lemon-dijon dressing.

Serves 4 · Prep: 20 min · Cook: 30–35 min

GF Vegetarian High Fibre Meal prep OK

Ingredients

Roasted veg
  • 5–6 medium carrots, cut into batons
  • 1 red onion, sliced into wedges
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
Lentil base
  • 1 1/2 cups dry green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bay leaf (optional)
  • Salt, to taste
Lemon-dijon dressing
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp maple syrup or honey
  • Salt and pepper
To finish
  • 2–3 tbsp chopped parsley
  • Crumbled feta or vegan feta (optional)

Method

  1. Heat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss carrots and onion with olive oil, salt, and pepper on a lined tray. Roast 25–30 minutes, until browned and tender.
  2. Meanwhile, combine lentils, broth/water, garlic, and bay leaf in a pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer 20–25 minutes until tender. Season with salt; drain excess liquid if needed.
  3. Whisk dressing ingredients in a small jar or bowl.
  4. To serve, scoop lentils into bowls, top with roasted veg, drizzle with dressing, and finish with parsley and feta if using.

Flourless Chocolate Almond Cake

Rich, fudgy, naturally gluten-free chocolate cake made with ground almonds.

Serves 8 · Prep: 20 min · Bake: 25–30 min

Ingredients

  • 200 g dark chocolate (ensure GF), chopped
  • 150 g butter or coconut oil
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 4 large eggs, room temp
  • 1½ cups fine almond flour
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Method

  1. Heat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line and grease an 8–9 in (20–23 cm) springform pan.
  2. Melt chocolate and butter together gently; cool slightly.
  3. Whisk sugar and eggs until slightly thickened.
  4. Stir in melted chocolate mixture, then almond flour, cocoa, vanilla, and salt.
  5. Pour into pan; bake 25–30 minutes until just set in the centre. Cool before slicing.
