Thai-Inspired Coconut Rice Veggie Bowls

Coconut rice with crunchy veg, peanuts, and a lime-chili drizzle.

Serves 4 · Prep: 20 min · Cook: 20 min

Gluten-Free Vegetarian Coconut rice Meal-prep OK

Ingredients

Coconut rice
  • 1½ cups jasmine rice, rinsed
  • 1 can (400 ml) coconut milk
  • 1 cup water
  • ½ tsp salt
Veg & toppings
  • 1 cup shredded carrot
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1 red pepper, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup roasted peanuts, roughly chopped
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
Lime-chili drizzle
  • 3 tbsp lime juice
  • 2 tbsp tamari (GF soy sauce)
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp chili-garlic sauce or sriracha

Method

  1. In a medium pot, combine the jasmine rice, coconut milk, water, and salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 12–15 minutes until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and let stand, covered, for 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork.
  2. While the rice cooks, whisk together all lime-chili drizzle ingredients in a small bowl: lime juice, tamari, maple syrup, and chili-garlic sauce or sriracha. Taste and tweak sweetness, salt, or heat as you like.
  3. Prep the veg and toppings: shred the carrot and cabbage, thinly slice the red pepper, roughly chop the peanuts, and chop the cilantro.
  4. Build the bowls: spoon a generous portion of coconut rice into each bowl. Top with carrot, cabbage, red pepper, peanuts, and cilantro.
  5. Drizzle each bowl with the lime-chili sauce right before serving. Serve extra drizzle on the side for anyone who wants more.
Nutrition & usage ▼

How to use it:

  • Great base to pair with any of your curry recipes.
  • For extra protein, add crispy tofu, edamame, or a soft-boiled egg on top.
  • Mixed-diet tweak: non-veg person can add grilled shrimp, chicken satay, or leftover roast meat to their bowl only; base stays fully veg and GF.
  • Meal prep: coconut rice will firm up in the fridge — reheat with a splash of water or extra coconut milk to loosen.

Roasted Miso Mushroom Brown Rice Bowls

Deep umami mushrooms, garlicky greens, and nutty brown rice.

Serves 4 · Prep 15 min · Cook 30 min

Gluten-Free Vegetarian High umami Meal-prep approved

Ingredients

Roasted miso mushrooms
  • 500 g cremini or mixed mushrooms, quartered
  • 2 tbsp white miso paste
  • 1 tbsp tamari
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, grated
Greens
  • 4 cups chopped kale or Swiss chard
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Pinch of salt
Base
  • 2 cups cooked brown rice
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Sesame seeds (garnish)

Method

  1. Heat oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a sheet pan.
  2. Whisk miso, tamari, maple syrup, sesame oil, and grated garlic. Toss mushrooms in mixture and spread on the pan.
  3. Roast 18–22 minutes until deep golden with caramelized edges.
  4. Sauté greens in olive oil and garlic until softened (3–4 min).
  5. Assemble: brown rice → greens → roasted mushrooms → green onions → sesame seeds.
Nutrition & usage ▼

How to use it:

  • Excellent with a jammy egg on top.
  • Add chili crisp for heat.
  • Keeps well in meal prep all week.

Crispy Tofu Sushi Bowls

All the best parts of sushi, none of the rolling.

Serves 4 · Prep 20 min · Cook 20 min

Gluten-Free Vegetarian Crispy tofu High protein

Ingredients

Tofu
  • 400 g extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp tamari
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil
Rice & toppings
  • 2 cups cooked sushi rice
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 carrot, shredded
  • 1 sheet nori, cut into strips
  • Pickled ginger
Drizzle
  • 2 tbsp mayo (or vegan mayo)
  • 1 tsp sriracha
  • 1 tsp lime juice

Method

  1. Toss tofu with cornstarch, tamari, and oil. Air-fry or oven-roast at 425°F for 20 minutes until crisp.
  2. Whisk drizzle ingredients.
  3. Assemble the bowls: rice → tofu → veg → nori.
  4. Finish with the spicy mayo drizzle.
Nutrition & usage ▼
  • Great cold-lunch option.
  • Easily boosted with edamame for more protein.
  • Use seasoned rice vinegar on rice for true sushi flavour.

Chipotle Sweet Potato & Black Bean Bowls

Smoky, sweet, spicy, fresh — the perfect balanced bowl.

Serves 4 · 30 minutes

Gluten-Free Vegetarian High fibre Meal prep

Ingredients

Sweet potatoes
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, cubed
  • 2 tsp chipotle in adobo, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt
Black beans
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
Fresh toppings
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Lime wedges
Base
  • Cooked rice or quinoa

Method

  1. Heat oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss sweet potatoes with chipotle, olive oil, and salt. Roast on a sheet pan for 25 minutes until browned and tender.
  2. Warm black beans in a small pan with cumin and smoked paprika, stirring until fragrant.
  3. Assemble bowls: base grain → black beans → sweet potato → avocado → corn → cilantro.
  4. Serve with lime wedges for squeezing over the top.
Nutrition & usage ▼
  • High-fibre, very filling lunch or dinner.
  • Optional extras: salsa verde, crumbled feta (if not fully vegan).

Lemon Herb Quinoa Greek Bowls

Bright, clean, high-protein quinoa with Mediterranean toppings.

Serves 4 · 25 minutes

Gluten-Free Vegetarian Fresh & bright

Ingredients

Quinoa
  • 1½ cups quinoa, rinsed
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp salt (or to taste)
Toppings
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, chopped
  • ½ red onion, thinly sliced
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • ½ cup crumbled feta (optional)
Dressing
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 clove garlic, grated
  • Pinch of salt and pepper

Method

  1. Cook quinoa according to package directions. While still warm, stir in lemon zest, lemon juice, olive oil, and salt.
  2. Whisk dressing ingredients until emulsified.
  3. Assemble bowls: quinoa → tomatoes → cucumber → red onion → parsley → feta.
  4. Drizzle with dressing just before serving.
Nutrition & usage ▼
  • Light but filling; great warm or room temperature.
  • Add chickpeas for extra protein and fibre.

Peanut-Ginger Rice Noodle Bowls

Creamy, spicy, sweet, savoury — the perfect noodle bowl.

Serves 4 · 20 minutes

Gluten-Free Vegetarian High flavour

Ingredients

Sauce
  • 3 tbsp peanut butter
  • 1½ tbsp tamari
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
  • Water, to thin as needed
Noodles & veg
  • 250 g rice noodles
  • 1 red pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • ¼ cup peanuts, crushed

Method

  1. Whisk sauce ingredients together, adding water gradually until smooth and pourable.
  2. Cook rice noodles according to package instructions. Rinse briefly in cold water to prevent sticking.
  3. Assemble: noodles → veg → sauce → green onions → crushed peanuts.
  4. Serve immediately, or keep sauce separate for meal prep to avoid soggy noodles.
Nutrition & usage ▼
  • Very popular meal-prep option.
  • Add crispy tofu or edamame for more protein.

Winter Roasted Veggie Tahini Bowls

Creamy, nutty tahini over caramelized cold-weather vegetables.

Serves 4 · 35–40 minutes

Gluten-Free Vegetarian Comforting

Ingredients

Roasted veg
  • 2 cups cubed winter squash (butternut, kabocha, etc.)
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
Tahini drizzle
  • 3 tbsp tahini
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp maple syrup
  • 1 small garlic clove, grated
  • Water, as needed to loosen
Base
  • Cooked quinoa or rice
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Optional: 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed

Method

  1. Heat oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss squash, cauliflower, and red onion with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on a sheet pan and roast 30 minutes, stirring once, until browned and tender.
  2. Whisk tahini, lemon juice, maple syrup, and garlic. Thin with water until smooth and drizzleable.
  3. If using chickpeas, you can toss them onto the pan for the last 10 minutes of roasting to crisp slightly.
  4. Assemble bowls: grain → roasted veg (and chickpeas, if using) → tahini drizzle → parsley.
Nutrition & usage ▼
  • Perfect cold-weather bowl; very satisfying.
  • Chickpeas add protein and make it more meal-prep friendly.

Golden Lentil Coconut Stew

Rich, creamy, turmeric-spiced lentils with a hint of lime.

Serves 4–5 · 35 minutes

Gluten-Free High protein Comfort

Ingredients

Base
  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
Broth
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • 1 can (400 ml) coconut milk
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • Salt to taste
Toppings
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Chili flakes (optional)

Method

  1. Sauté onion, garlic, and ginger in olive oil over medium heat until softened, 4–5 minutes.
  2. Stir in turmeric, cumin, and paprika; cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add lentils, coconut milk, and vegetable broth. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  4. Simmer 18–22 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lentils are soft and stew is thickened.
  5. Stir in lime juice and adjust salt to taste. Thin with a splash of water or broth if too thick.
  6. Serve hot, topped with cilantro and chili flakes.
Nutrition & usage ▼

How to use it:

  • Very filling on its own; also great over rice.
  • Add spinach in the last 5 minutes for extra greens.
  • Good for 3–4 days in the fridge; thickens as it sits.

Hearty Potato & Corn Chowder

Creamy, cozy, smoky flavour without meat.

Serves 4 · 30 minutes

Gluten-Free Vegetarian Comfort food

Ingredients

Base
  • 3 medium potatoes, diced small
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil or butter
Broth
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup milk or unsweetened coconut milk
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • Salt & black pepper
Finish
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Shredded cheddar (optional, not vegan)

Method

  1. In a pot, sauté onion, carrot, and celery in oil over medium heat until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add potatoes, corn, vegetable broth, smoked paprika, thyme, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a simmer.
  3. Simmer 12–15 minutes until potatoes are very tender.
  4. Stir in milk. Use a potato masher to roughly mash some of the potatoes in the pot to thicken the chowder.
  5. Simmer another 3–5 minutes; adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve topped with green onions and cheddar, if using.
Nutrition & usage ▼

How to use it:

  • Serious “comfort” energy; serve with a simple green salad.
  • Use smoked paprika generously for a “bacon-y” note without meat.

Umami Mushroom “Barley” Soup (GF)

Classic barley soup flavour, recreated with gluten-free grains.

Serves 4 · 40 minutes

Gluten-Free Deep umami Vegetarian

Ingredients

Base
  • 1 cup short-grain brown rice or sorghum (rinsed)
  • 400 g mixed mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
Broth
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp tamari (GF soy sauce)
  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste
Aromatics
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Method

  1. In a large pot, sauté mushrooms over medium-high heat (dry or with a splash of oil) until deeply browned and they’ve released most of their moisture.
  2. Add olive oil, onion, carrot, celery, and garlic. Cook 4–5 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in brown rice, vegetable broth, tamari, thyme, and pepper. Bring to a simmer.
  4. Simmer 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until rice is tender. Add a splash more water if it gets too thick.
  5. Adjust salt to taste before serving.
Nutrition & usage ▼

How to use it:

  • Deep, savoury “meaty” flavour without any meat.
  • Good base soup to pair with a sandwich or big salad.

Tuscan White Bean & Kale Soup

Savory, herby, rustic Italian-style bean soup.

Serves 4 · 30 minutes

High protein Gluten-Free Vegetarian

Ingredients

Base
  • 2 cans cannellini or navy beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 2 cups chopped kale (stems removed)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
Broth
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp dried rosemary (crushed between fingers)
  • Salt & black pepper
Finish
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Grated Parmesan (optional, not vegan)

Method

  1. Sauté onion, carrot, and celery in olive oil over medium heat until softened, 5–6 minutes.
  2. Add beans, vegetable broth, oregano, rosemary, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  3. Simmer 10–12 minutes so flavours meld.
  4. Stir in kale and cook 3–5 minutes until tender but bright.
  5. Finish with lemon juice, then adjust salt and pepper.
  6. Serve with Parmesan on top if using.
Nutrition & usage ▼

How to use it:

  • High in fibre and protein; good main meal by itself.
  • Super forgiving for meal prep — reheats very well.

Spicy Peanut Noodle Soup

Creamy, spicy broth with rice noodles and greens.

Serves 4 · 20 minutes

Gluten-Free High flavour Vegetarian

Ingredients

Broth
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp peanut butter
  • 1 tbsp tamari
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1–2 tsp chili-garlic sauce (to taste)
Noodles & veg
  • 200 g rice noodles
  • 1 red pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup baby spinach
  • 2 green onions, sliced

Method

  1. In a pot, whisk together broth, coconut milk, peanut butter, tamari, lime juice, and chili-garlic sauce.
  2. Bring to a gentle simmer, whisking until peanut butter is fully dissolved.
  3. Add rice noodles and red pepper; simmer until noodles are just tender (check package time).
  4. Stir in spinach and cook 1 minute until just wilted.
  5. Serve immediately, topped with green onions.
Nutrition & usage ▼

How to use it:

  • Big flavour, fairly energy-dense; a full meal in a bowl.
  • Add crispy tofu or edamame on top for extra protein.

Roasted Tomato Basil Soup

Deep roasted tomato flavour with garlic and fresh basil.

Serves 4 · 40 minutes

Gluten-Free Vegetarian Roasted flavour

Ingredients

Roast
  • 6 large ripe tomatoes, halved
  • 1 small onion, quartered
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt & black pepper
Blend
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • ½ cup fresh basil leaves, loosely packed
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • ½ tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)

Method

  1. Heat oven to 425°F (220°C). Place tomatoes, onion, and garlic on a sheet pan; drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Roast 25–30 minutes until tomatoes are soft and edges are starting to caramelize.
  3. Transfer roasted veg (including juices) to a blender with vegetable broth, basil, tomato paste, and sugar if using. Blend until smooth.
  4. Pour into a pot and simmer 8–10 minutes to deepen the flavour. Adjust salt and pepper.
  5. Serve hot with extra basil on top.
Nutrition & usage ▼

How to use it:

  • Light but flavourful; pair with a GF grilled cheese or big salad.
  • Freezes well for quick future lunches.
